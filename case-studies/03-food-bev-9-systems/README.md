## The Situation

A regional food and beverage brand with a proven concept was preparing for multi-location expansion. The owner had deep product expertise but no documented systems — operations, fulfillment, and marketing all lived in their head.

Without a scalable operating model, every new location would inherit the same chaos at a higher cost.

---

## The System

MAXXAM AI applied the **9-Systems Framework** to map every core business function:

1. Lead Generation
2. Lead Conversion
3. Client Onboarding
4. Service Delivery
5. Retention & Upsell
6. Referral
7. Financial Operations
8. Team & Vendor Management
9. Marketing & Visibility

Each system was documented, then connected to automation where possible.

**Deployed automations:**
- **Central Activity Logger** (n8n) — tracks all revenue and operational events in real time
- **Performance Engine** — surfaces daily dashboards for data-driven management

**Stack:** n8n · Google Sheets · Google Data Studio · Gmail

---

## Architecture Overview

```
9-Systems Map
    ↓
[System Documentation] → Standard Operating Procedures
    ↓
[Automation Layer]
  Central Activity Logger (n8n)
      ↓
  Google Sheets (data warehouse)
      ↓
  Performance Dashboard
      ↓
  Daily Report (email)
```

---

## The Takeaway

Most expansion failures happen because the business scaled before the systems did. A second location doesn't fix the first location's problems — it multiplies them.

The 9-Systems framework forces the work most operators skip: documenting and automating the operation so it runs without the founder's constant presence.
