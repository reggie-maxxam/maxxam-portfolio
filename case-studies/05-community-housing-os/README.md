# Case Study 05 — Community Housing Business OS

**Industry:** Community Development / Affordable Housing  
**System Type:** Operations & Strategy · Business OS + Grant Strategy  
**Published:** June 2026

---

## Results

| Metric | Outcome |
|--------|---------|
| Automation flows mapped | **12** |

| Operational segments structured | **3** |

---

## The Situation

A mission-driven organization developing an affordable housing community had a compelling vision — but no operational structure to match. Plans lived in email threads and founder conversations.

Without documented systems, tracking, or reporting infrastructure:
- Grant funding was out of reach (no demonstrated operational maturity)
- Consistent service delivery was impossible at scale
- Leadership was the bottleneck for every decision

---

## The System

MAXXAM AI built a complete **Business OS** across three resident segments:

**Operational Architecture:**
- 3-segment service delivery model with distinct workflows per segment
- 12 automation flows mapped (communication, intake, reporting, outreach)
- Standard operating procedures for each core function

**CEO Dashboard Suite (5 dashboards):**
1. Lead pipeline and conversion tracking
2. Unit availability and occupancy
3. Revenue and financial performance
4. Capacity planning
5. Grant and funding pipeline

**Grant Strategy:**
- Funding source research and prioritization
- Application positioning framework (operational maturity narrative)
- Documentation structured to support external funding applications

**Stack:** Google Sheets · Google Data Studio · n8n (mapped, not yet deployed) · Notion

---

## Deliverables

- Business OS proposal document (full architecture)
- Community marketing brochure (resident-facing)
- Teaser summary (investor/funder-facing)
- Grant funding strategy
- CEO dashboard wireframes
- Phased implementation roadmap (Q1 2027 target)

---

## The Takeaway

For mission-driven organizations, operational clarity IS the mission. A community that can't measure its own performance can't prove it deserves funding.

The Business OS isn't overhead — it's what makes the mission sustainable at scale, not just compelling on paper.
